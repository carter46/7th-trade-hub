## 1.0 Brand Introduction

### Brand Strategy

#### Vision

To be the premier destination in Plateau State, seamlessly blending the rustic charm of its rich heritage with the sophistication of modern amenities to provide a unique and memorable experience for both business and leisure travelers.

#### Mission

To offer exceptional hospitality that harmoniously integrates the warmth of Plateau's culture with unparalleled service excellence, thereby creating lasting memories and fostering strong community connections.

#### Positioning

The Hill Station Hotel is positioned as a gateway to the adventure and culture of Plateau State, providing a sanctuary of comfort where history meets contemporary elegance.

Designed with the theme of "Rustic Elegance," the hotel offers a unique blend of traditional charm and modern amenities, targeting adventure seekers and business travelers alike. It serves as the perfect base for exploring the beauty and diversity of the region while ensuring every guest feels at home.

### Brand Values

- Heritage and Innovation: Embracing rich history while driving forward with innovative hospitality solutions.
- Community and Inclusion: Building strong, positive relationships with the local community and ensuring an inclusive environment for all guests and staff.
- Sustainability and Responsibility: Committing to sustainable practices and responsible stewardship of environmental and cultural resources.

### Brand Attributes

The brand connects heritage and culture with comfort, tranquility, and rejuvenation.

Core attributes:

- Authentic
- Cultured
- Adventurous
- Rejuvenating
- Welcoming
- Timeless
- Immersive

Supporting description:

Some attributes capture the brand's connection to heritage and culture, such as Authentic, Cultured, and Timeless. Others highlight comfort, tranquility, and rejuvenating experiences, such as Serene, Soulful, and Rejuvenating. Adventurous and Immersive speak to discovery and cultural immersion. Welcoming reflects warm hospitality and the seamless integration of tradition with modernity.

### Brand Tagline

The tagline is the memorable statement attached to brand touchpoints to inspire and engage audiences.

Main tagline identified by OCR:

> Where Comfort Meets Adventure

Associated themes:

- Culture
- Opportunity
- Relaxation
- Tranquility

Note: The OCR also picked up some distorted versions of the tagline on visual mockup pages. Confirm the final approved tagline in the original PDF artwork before publishing.

### Tone of Voice

The brand voice should feel:

- Relaxed
- Playful
- Assured
- Evocative
- Conversational
- Heartfelt

The voice should avoid feeling:

- Formal
- Serious
- Modest
- Simple
- Institutional
- Clinical

## 2.0 Brand Logo

### Brand Logo

The logo is the brand's first impression. Applied consistently, it repeats the brand promise and helps create a memorable visual identity.

### Logo Lockup

The logo combines a unique symbol with a bespoke wordmark.

The symbol is inspired by scenic rock formations. It features a rock-like shape with five points, representing five-star excellence. It also depicts an optical illusion of sunrise, symbolizing a new beginning.

The wordmark "the hill station" is distinct and elegant, complementing the symbol. When paired together, the symbol and wordmark create the logo, which should be used consistently.

### Primary Vertical Logo

The logo is used in nearly all communications. It is the brand's stamp of authenticity.

Use the correct logo in every situation and keep it consistent, legible, and identifiable.

The vertical version is the preferred logo and should always be the first choice over the horizontal logo.

### Construction - Vertical Logo

The logo has been carefully crafted to read well, even at small sizes. There is no limit at large scale, but care should be taken at smaller sizes.

Minimum size:

- Vertical logo: 200px

### Clearspace - Vertical Logo

The exclusion zone keeps the logo legible and impactful by isolating it from competing visual elements such as text and supporting graphics.

The exclusion zone is equal to the x-height of the wordmark, defined as the height of the "i" without the tittle/dot.

This zone is the absolute minimum safe distance. In most cases, the logo should be given more room to breathe.

### Secondary Horizontal Logo

The horizontal logo is reserved for special uses. It should only be used when limited space prevents use of the vertical logo.

### Construction - Horizontal Logo

The horizontal logo has been carefully crafted to read well at small sizes. Follow the minimum size requirement when using it at small scale.

Minimum size:

- Horizontal logo: 200px

### Clearspace - Horizontal Logo

Use the same exclusion-zone principle as the vertical logo: spacing should be equal to the x-height of the wordmark, treated as the absolute minimum safe distance.

### Logo Mark

The logo mark is a distinct symbol or graphic element that can stand alone without the brand name or wordmark.

The lines depict a beam of light from sunrise, signifying a new dawn, while the five hill points signify five-star service.

### Construction - Logo Mark

The logo mark has been crafted to read well, even in small sizes. Use the minimum size requirement at small scale.

Minimum size:

- Logo mark: 90px

### Clearspace - Logo Mark

The logo mark should be isolated from competing visual elements. Use the x-height of the wordmark as the minimum exclusion zone, and give the mark more space where possible.

### Background Applications

When overlaying the logo on brand colors or photography, always ensure there is ample contrast. The guide includes approved visual combinations for different backgrounds.

### Logo Co-branding

When positioning dual logos in brand partnerships, use the same principles that apply to clearspace.

Use the x-height of the Hill Station wordmark for both logos, and use a 1pt rule extending to the top and bottom of the symbol height as a divider between the logos.

Lockup types shown:

- Primary partnership lockup
- Multiple partnership lockup
- Powered by lockup

### Logo Window

The symbol can be used as a window or image mask in communications.

Images placed inside the symbol require careful consideration so that they work within the unique shape. Landscape images typically work best.

### Logo Usage

When applying the logo to physical objects such as clothing or buildings, make sure it remains clear and legible from a distance.

### Logo Misuse

Avoid the following:

- Do not stretch the logo.
- Do not apply shadows or effects.
- Do not outline the logo.
- Do not rotate the logo.
- Do not use unapproved colors.
- Do not apply patterns to the logo.

### Creative Usage

Minimum color:

For functional pieces like technical diagrams, use a reduced palette. Cloud and Sand work as base colors to improve clarity and legibility, while occasional colors can bring attention to important information.

Maximum color:

### Color with Photography

When choosing a color to pair with photography, look for items in the image that closely match a color in the palette. This creates the most harmonious pairing.

Always ensure enough contrast between the image and background color.

When there is no matching color in the image, choose a more neutral color that does not feel jarring. Blues, greens, and Sand are recommended neutral pairings.

## 4.0 Brand Typography

### Primary Typography

Primary typeface:

- Newyork

Newyork is the primary brand typeface. In headlines, it gives Hill Station an elegant typographic voice and differentiates the brand from other companies and organizations.

Use Newyork for:

- Headlines
- Some subheadlines

Newyork family:

- Newyork Regular
- Newyork Medium
- Newyork Bold

### Secondary Typography

Secondary typeface:

- Navigo

Navigo is a modern sans-serif typeface created for maximum flexibility in communication. Its refined aesthetic and wide range of widths make it an ideal supporting typeface.

Use Navigo for:

- Body copy
- CTAs
- Many subheadlines
- Subcopy

Navigo family:

- Navigo Thin
- Navigo Light
- Navigo Regular
- Navigo Medium
- Navigo Bold
- Navigo Black

### Typography Hierarchy

Size, scale, and position affect how information is read. Ensure a purposeful difference between type sizes.

Recommended hierarchy:

- Eyebrows: Navigo Semibold, uppercase, about 1/6 of headline size, tracking 0, leading 0.
- Headlines: Newyork Regular, uppercase, tracking 0, leading 0.
- Subheadlines: Navigo Regular, sentence case, about 1/3 of headline size, tracking 0, leading 0.
- Body copy: Navigo Regular, sentence case, about 1/5 of headline size when subheadlines are present.
- Button font: Navigo Semibold, uppercase, about 1/8 of headline size, tracking 0, leading 0.

### Typography Alignment

When clarity is imperative, left-aligned text is the default.

In more expressive brand materials, type may be offset in more interesting ways.

### Typography Layout

The guide shows layout examples using:

- Eyebrows at roughly 1/6 headline size
- Headlines in strong contrast
- Subheadlines at roughly 1/3 headline size
- Body copy set smaller for readability
- Button font for CTAs

Three common formats are recommended and should be used appropriately:

Layout guidance:

## 6.0 Brand Media

### Brand Photography

Hill Station photography should capture the brand's perspective of the world, both literally and figuratively. It should show nature, authenticity, and community.

### Photography Principles

Hill Station imagery should be cohesive and high quality, guided by three principles:

1. Optimistic
2. Authentic
3. Dynamic

Use these principles as a checklist when capturing photos or selecting imagery for the brand.

All photographs should have an editorial feel. They should capture a moment, tell a story, and feel natural. They should not appear overly stock or disingenuous.

### Principle: Optimistic

Hill Station is about comfort meeting diverse experiences, and the imagery should reflect this.

The brand embraces an optimistic tone by appearing human and welcoming. Images should not feel cold or too dark.

Key elements:

- Warm, natural lighting
- Vibrant tones
- Human-centric composition
- Intimate cropping
- Positive facial expressions
- Welcoming and relaxed poses

### Principle: Authentic

Hill Station is a real company providing real services to real people, so imagery should capture real situations.

Photography should show people indulging in new experiences and should document real people in real situations.

Key elements:

- Authentic, candid moments in a journalistic style
- Images that tell a story
- Natural styling
- Attention to detail

### Principle: Dynamic

Dynamic imagery should not be predictable; it should feel adventurous.

Use high and low angles to allude to the nature of the environment. Use a mix of macro and micro shots to reflect Jos's beautiful landscape.

Key elements:

- Macro and micro shots showing scale at different levels
- Complexity from close detail shots to wider landscapes
- Different perspectives, including in the air and on the ground
- Interesting and unexpected cropping or framing

## Practical Website Notes

For the website, the strongest usable brand direction from the guide is:

- Position Hill Station as a gateway to Plateau State adventure, culture, and comfort.
- Use "Rustic Elegance" as the design and messaging theme.
- Lean into heritage, community, sustainability, and five-star service.
- Use the tagline "Where Comfort Meets Adventure" after confirming it visually in the original guide.
- Prefer the vertical logo; use horizontal only when space is tight.
- Use Forest, Earth, Sky, and Sand as the core palette, with Cloud and Charcoal as functional neutrals.
- Use Newyork for elegant headline moments and Navigo for body copy, navigation, CTAs, forms, and UI text.
- Use warm, natural, human, editorial photography of Jos, Plateau landscapes, hospitality moments, rooms, food, and guest experiences.
- Avoid cold, stock-like, overly dark, or overly formal visuals and copy.
